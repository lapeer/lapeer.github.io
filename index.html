Research

My current research is rooted in galactic dynamics. I have been working alongside several members of the Galactic Dynamics Group at the University of Michigan along with a cohort of international collaborators to constrain the central SMBH mass of 18 compact stellar systems in the Virgo cluster; the first census of it's kind. We have obtained observations of 18 with the JWST NIRSpec IFU such systems and are currently working on applying the Schwarzschild method of dynamical modeling to detect and constrain the mass of each systems SMBH.

My role in this project has been determining the lower limit of detectable SMBH mass in these systems. By constructing large scale batches of "mock galaxies", we were able to generate observations representative of the true observations and probe the lower limit of detectable SMBH mass. We can now confidently say that a SMBH with mass greater than ~1% of the systems stellar mass are detectable.

Now, I am working alongside Zach Sunmers at the University of Calgary to tailor the JWST data reduction pipeline to best suit our needs.
