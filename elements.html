Teaching and Outreach

Michigan Math and Science Scholars (MMSS)

During the summer of 2023, I had the opportunity to help facilitate a two week program through the Michigan Math and Science (MMSS) program. The course, titled “Hunting for the Dark: Black Holes and Dark Matter”, gave high school students from around the world the opportunity to learn the basics of astronomy and astrophysics, with an emphasis on dark matter and black holes. My role was the facilitation of daily programming labs, in which the students learned the basics of programming in the context of astronomy. I also created a final project for the students to do, in which they delved into the M-sigma relationship with TNG-100 data.

Astronomy Monthly at WCC

Through my role as a peer tutor at Washtenaw Community College, I have been able to implement a monthly workshop titled “Astronomy Monthly”. Beginning in September of 2023, the workshops are designed to engage students from any sort of background with astronomy. Our first meeting focused on accessible observing methods, and highlighted many local resources for observing for free! Accessible astronomy is an important topic to me, and engaging non-traditional students is an area of outreach that can traditionally be left out of the conversation. When I complete my studies in Ann Arbor, the program will be taken over by the Student Astronomical Society at the University of Michigan, ensuring that non-traditional students remain engaged with the field.

Peer Tutor at Washtenaw Community College

Since summer 2022, I have been a peer tutor of math and physics at Washtenaw Community College. As a community college transfer student, engaging with individuals from non-traditional backgrounds is incredibly important to me. In academia (and more specifically, astronomy and physics) students from such backgrounds are not included in the traditional conversations regarding outreach and DEI. One of my key career goals is to change this, and through my position at WCC I have been able to do just that.

SAS Mentorship Program

Starting fall 2023, I have had the privilege of participating in the Student Astronomical Society Mentorship Program. Allan VanZandt (another UM undergraduate) and I have mentored three 1st-2nd year undergraduate astronomy students. The program was recently revived and aims to help underclassmen navigate the field of astronomy. Mentor-mentee pairs were selected based on research interests, with Allan and I running the Galaxies/Black Holes/Near Universe Cosmology group.

Telescope Operator at the University of Michigan

As of summer 2023, I am a telescope operator for the University of Michigan. This role involves operating our on-campus observatory for several introductory astronomy courses and Student Astronomical Society open houses. Through both of those, I have been able to engage with students outside of the field who have a passing interest in astronomy, or community members just looking to learn something new.
